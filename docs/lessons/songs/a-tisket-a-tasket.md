# SONG: A Tisket A Tasket

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
A tisket, a tasket, a green and yellow basket
I wrote a letter to my love and on the way I lost it
I lost it, I lost it, and on the way I lost it
A little girl picked it up and put it in her pocket

**Verse 2:**
A tisket, a tasket, a green and yellow basket
She took my letter from the ground and never did she ask it
She asked me, she asked me, how could she have asked me?
A tisket, a tasket, a green and yellow basket

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **BASKET** - Form both hands into O-shapes (thumbs and fingers joined) and position them in front of you with palms facing forward. Move them outward and slightly upward while opening the fingers into spread hands, showing the shape and opening of a basket.

- **LETTER** - Hold your non-dominant hand flat and open in front of you. With your dominant hand, touch your fingers to your lips (as if they've been moistened by licking), then touch them to the palm of your non-dominant hand, miming the action of sealing a letter.

- **LOVE** - Cross both hands over your heart and then move them outward slightly while pressing palms toward your chest, showing affection and care.

- **LOST** - Hold both hands up with palms facing forward and fingers spread. Shake them back and forth in a confused manner, showing the feeling of being lost.

- **FIND** - Hold your non-dominant hand still with fingers spread. Move your dominant hand across it, ending with your thumb and index finger pinching as if picking something up from the palm, showing the action of finding something.

- **GIRL** - First sign BOY (tap cheek with four fingers moving downward), then sign WOMAN (open hand on cheek moving down). For just GIRL, tap the side of your cheek once or twice with your thumb.

- **POCKET** - Tap your chest or side of your body repeatedly with all fingers extended, showing where a pocket would be.

## Song Overview

"A Tisket A Tasket" is a whimsical nursery rhyme that tells a simple story about a lost letter and a helpful friend. Young children love the tongue-twister quality of the opening phrase and the engaging narrative that unfolds with each verse. The song develops memory and sequencing skills as children follow the story, while the repetitive, catchy melody makes it easy to learn and sing alongâ€”ideal for children ages 2-4+ who are beginning to understand cause and effect and appreciate gentle storytelling through song.

**Category**: Nursery Rhymes

**Origin**: This nursery rhyme is believed to date back to the 1600s or earlier, with roots in English and European folk traditions. The exact authorship is unknown, but the rhyme became widely popular through publication in children's books and songbooks starting in the 1800s. It was notably recorded and popularized in the 20th century through various children's recordings and television programs, becoming a classic of early childhood education.

**Duration**: About 1-2 minutes

## Cultural Notes

"A Tisket A Tasket" has been a beloved nursery rhyme in English-speaking countries for generations, particularly in the United States and United Kingdom. The rhyme has inspired various adaptations, including a famous 1938 version by jazz singer Ella Fitzgerald that added to its cultural prominence. In playground games and school settings, the rhyme is often used as an action song where children sit in a circle and play a version of the game "Duck, Duck, Goose" using this song. The whimsical language and gentle story make it easy to translate and adapt across cultures, and it remains a staple in preschools and daycare centers worldwide.
