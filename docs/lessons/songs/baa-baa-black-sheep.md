# SONG: Baa Baa Black Sheep

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
Baa, baa, black sheep,
Have you any wool?
Yes, sir, yes, sir,
Three bags full.

**Verse 2:**
One for my master,
One for my dame,
And one for the little boy
Who lives down the lane.

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **Black** - Move your index finger across your forehead from left to right, showing darkness
- **Sheep** - Bring both hands to your shoulders and wiggle your fingers down your arms to show woolly, curly fleece
- **Wool** - Rub your fingers together in front of your chest as if feeling soft, fluffy texture
- **Bags** - Make both hands into fists with thumbs hooked, then pull outward and downward to show bags being held by handles
- **Master** - Point upward with both index fingers, then make the sign for "man" by placing your hand on your forehead and moving it downward
- **Boy** - Make a "C" shape with your hand at your forehead level, then move it downward while opening your fingers to show a boy growing
- **Three** - Hold up your index, middle, and ring fingers to show the number three
- **Lane** - Point with both index fingers outward and move them forward parallel to each other, showing a path or road

## Song Overview

"Baa Baa Black Sheep" is a beloved counting and naming song that introduces children to animals, colors, and quantities through an unforgettable melody that also serves as the tune for the alphabet song and "Twinkle Twinkle Little Star." The interactive question-and-answer format actively engages children and encourages participation and response. The simple, repetitive lyrics and shared melody with other learning songs make it an efficient tool for early language development and concept learning for children ages 2-4+.

**Category**: Nursery Rhymes

**Origin**: "Baa Baa Black Sheep" traces back to at least 1731 and may be inspired by the English poem "The Fleece" by John Philips written in 1726. The melody is shared with the alphabet song "ABC Song" and "Twinkle Twinkle Little Star," which was originally a French melody later adapted by English speakers. This connection makes the song highly efficient for young learners who recognize the familiar tune across multiple contexts.

**Duration**: About 1-2 minutes

## Cultural Notes

This song has delightful variations across different cultures and time periods. In some versions, the sheep is a different color or the wool goes to different recipients such as family members or specific characters. The French version "Petit Agneau" follows a similar pattern and melody. Modern adaptations sometimes extend the song with additional verses about other animals like cows, goats, or pigs.
