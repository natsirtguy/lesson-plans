## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **BABY** - Hold both hands in front of you at chest height, palms facing inward, and rock them side to side as if cradling an infant. This mimics the gentle rocking motion of holding a baby.

- **SHARK** - Place both hands together with fingers pointing forward to form a pointed mouth shape. Move your hands forward through the air in a swimming motion, keeping the shark mouth shape to represent a shark swimming through water.

- **MOMMY** - Tap your thumb on your chin twice with your right hand (the female sign), then bring both hands together in front of your chest and rock them side to side as if cradling a baby (the caregiver sign combined).

- **DADDY** - Tap your thumb on your forehead twice with your right hand (the male sign), then bring both hands together in front of your chest and rock them side to side like cradling a baby.

- **RUN** - Hold both arms bent at the elbows and move them forward and backward in alternating motions while moving your body forward. This mimics the natural motion of running or escaping quickly.

- **WATER** - Hold all four fingers of your right hand together and tap them on your chin (the initial sign for water), then move your hand downward to show water flowing down.

- **SWIM** - Make both hands flat like fins, place them together pointing forward, and move them in a swimming motion through the air in front of your body, mimicking fish swimming through water.

## Song Overview

Baby Shark is a wildly popular modern song that captivates young children with its simple, repetitive melody and silly "doo doo doo" refrain that they love to imitate. The song features family members and silly actions that children can act out with dancing and hand motions, making it both musically engaging and physically active. This song develops rhythm recognition, family vocabulary, motor skills through dancing, and creates joyful memories while building confidence in young singers who love performing for others.

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: Baby Shark was created in 2016 by Pinkfong, a South Korean edutainment company. The song went viral globally on social media platforms and has become one of the most popular children's songs of the 2020s, translated into multiple languages and performed by children worldwide in schools, daycare centers, and homes.

**Duration**: About 2-3 minutes for all six verses

## Cultural Notes

Baby Shark represents modern global children's music, created in South Korea but adopted by children and families worldwide. The song's viral dance has transcended cultural boundaries, with versions performed across continents by children of all backgrounds. Many cultures have adapted the song to include local family members or incorporate traditional dance movements from their own cultures to the familiar melody. The "doo doo doo" chorus is universally recognizable, making it easy for children learning multiple languages to participate and sing along regardless of their language background.
