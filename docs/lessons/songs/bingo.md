## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **FARMER** - Make both hands into fists at shoulder level and move them down and forward in a repetitive digging or sowing motion to show farming work.
- **DOG** - Snap your fingers or make a snapping motion with one hand, then open your mouth slightly showing teeth or panting to indicate a dog.
- **NAME** - Bring both index and middle fingers together and cross them at the side of your head near your temple, or tap your temple twice to indicate a name.
- **B** - Hold your hand in a B shape (fingers together, thumb tucked in) and move it in front of your body.
- **LETTER** (General) - Make a pinching motion with your thumb and index finger to show something small, representing a single letter or character.
- **BINGO** - Spell out B-I-N-G-O while signing each letter individually, using clear hand positions for each letter from A-Z in sign language alphabet.
- **CLAP** - Bring both hands together and clap them one or more times, showing the action of clapping.

## Song Overview

This beloved song is a perfect tool for teaching letter recognition and spelling while maintaining the musicality and fun that captures young children's attention. The progressive clapping game transforms the song into an interactive experience that builds anticipation and challenges developing memory skills as children learn to recognize which letters to clap instead of sing. The familiar storytelling framework—a farmer with a dog—provides context and meaning, while the repetitive structure and playful game element make it engaging for repeated singing. Children develop early literacy skills, auditory discrimination, and the ability to follow increasingly complex pattern changes across multiple verses.

**Category**: Alphabet & Learning

**Origin**: A traditional American children's song with folk roots, likely dating back to the 19th or early 20th century. The song became particularly popular in American schools as a teaching tool for letters and spelling. The melody is simple and memorable, making it ideal for educational contexts. The song has been used in schools and homes for generations as an engaging way to help children learn the alphabet and basic spelling.

**Duration**: About 2-3 minutes (singing through all verses with progressive claps)

## Cultural Notes

"B-I-N-G-O" exists in several cultural variations, with different animals or stories in some international versions. However, the spelling game element remains consistent and recognizable across English-speaking cultures worldwide. The song's structure makes it highly adaptable—caregivers can substitute different animal names and letters to create custom versions or extend learning to other concepts. This song is often used in classroom settings and has been featured in numerous children's educational programs and books. The clapping game variation teaches children that letters can be represented in different ways (spoken, clapped, or signed), supporting flexible thinking about language and communication.
