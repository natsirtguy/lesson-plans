# SONG: Five Little Ducks

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
Five little ducks went out one day
Over the hills and far away
Mother duck called "Quack, quack, quack, quack"
And four little ducks came back

**Verse 2:**
Four little ducks went out one day
Over the hills and far away
Mother duck called "Quack, quack, quack, quack"
And three little ducks came back

**Verse 3:**
Three little ducks went out one day
Over the hills and far away
Mother duck called "Quack, quack, quack, quack"
And two little ducks came back

**Verse 4:**
Two little ducks went out one day
Over the hills and far away
Mother duck called "Quack, quack, quack, quack"
And one little duck came back

**Verse 5:**
One little duck went out one day
Over the hills and far away
Mother duck called "Quack, quack, quack, quack"
And no little ducks came back

**Final Verse:**
Mother duck went out one day
Over the hills and far away
Mother duck called "Quack, quack, quack, quack"
And all five little ducks came back

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **DUCKS** - Hold both hands up with thumbs and index fingers pinched together like a duck's bill, and move hands forward and back in front of mouth to show quacking motion
- **SWIM** - Interlock fingers of both hands and move them in a swimming motion away from your body
- **HILL** - Hold one hand flat and curved upward to show the shape of a hill, then pass the other hand over it
- **FAR** - Hold both hands in front of you, then push them away from your body to show something being distant
- **MOTHER** - Tap your chin with your thumb while keeping other fingers extended, then bring both hands together to show togetherness
- **QUACK** - Make duck bill motion with thumb and fingers, opening and closing near your mouth
- **BACK** - Point backward with your thumb over your shoulder
- **CALL** - Bring one hand to your mouth and motion outward as if calling to someone

## Song Overview

"Five Little Ducks" is a classic counting-down song that delights toddlers with its repetitive structure and quacking sounds. This song beautifully supports number recognition, sequencing skills, and language development through its predictable pattern that children quickly learn to anticipate. The combination of counting, animal sounds, and a reassuring ending (when all ducks return) helps develop cognitive skills while the hand motions and rhythm work together to strengthen gross and fine motor coordination.

**Category**: Nature & Animal Songs

**Origin**: Traditional children's song with roots in various cultures, popularized worldwide through children's media and educational programs. The song's exact origins are unclear, but it became widely known in the 20th century as a standard nursery rhyme and counting song.

**Duration**: About 2-3 minutes
