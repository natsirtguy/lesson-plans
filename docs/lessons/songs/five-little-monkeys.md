## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **MONKEY** - Make clawed "5" handshapes and scratch under your arms on both sides, moving your hands up and down (showing a monkey scratching)
- **JUMPING** - Make "V" handshapes with both hands and bend them up and down, showing bouncing or jumping motion
- **BED** - Tilt your head to the side and rest it on your hand as if lying in bed, or make a rectangle shape with both hands to show a bed
- **FELL** - Hold one hand up and move it downward quickly, as if something falling
- **HEAD** - Touch the top of your head with one or both hands
- **DOCTOR** - Make a "D" handshape with one hand and tap your wrist (where you'd take a pulse) with your other hand, or use both hands to show examining a patient
- **MAMA** - Make an "M" handshape and tap your chin or cheek to indicate mother
- **NO/NOT** - Shake your head side to side while holding one hand up with your index and middle fingers crossed, or make an "X" shape with both hands

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: A modern nursery rhyme that became popular in the 20th century. While the specific author is unknown, the song gained widespread recognition in the mid-1900s and has since become a staple in early childhood education worldwide. **Duration**: About 2-3 minutes (for all five verses, including pauses for actions)

## Cultural Notes

"Five Little Monkeys" has become a global phenomenon and appears in many languages and cultural adaptations worldwide. While the monkey imagery is universal, some cultures have adapted the song to feature other animals or scenarios that better reflect their contexts. The song is available in countless YouTube versions, children's books, and educational programs internationally. Its popularity in schools and educational settings has made it one of the most recognized modern children's songs. The combination of counting, consequences, and comedy transcends cultural boundaries, making it accessible and entertaining for children everywhere.
