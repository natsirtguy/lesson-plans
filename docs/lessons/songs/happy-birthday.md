# SONG: Happy Birthday

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
Happy birthday to you,
Happy birthday to you,
Happy birthday, dear [Name],
Happy birthday to you.

**Verse 2 (Alternative ending):**
Happy birthday to you,
You smell like monkey poo,
You look like one too,
Happy birthday to you.

**Verse 3 (Another variation):**
How old are you now?
How old are you now?
You're [number] years old,
How old are you now?

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **HAPPY** - Place both hands on your chest with palms inward, then move them upward and outward with a smile, showing a happy/joyful expression.
- **BIRTHDAY** - Make a flat hand shape and place it over your heart, then move it outward; then make "B" shapes with both hands and rotate them inward like candles.
- **YOU** - Point directly at the person being celebrated with one or both index fingers.
- **SING** - Move one or both hands back and forth across your mouth and chin in a rhythmic motion.
- **CAKE** - Make a "C" shape with one hand and place it on your other open palm, then move your hands apart to show the shape of a cake.
- **CANDLES** - Hold up all ten fingers with palms facing each other, wiggling them slightly to show flickering flames.
- **CLAP** - Bring both hands together repeatedly in a clapping motion to show celebration and applause.
- **CELEBRATE** - Make both hands into fists, raise them to shoulder height, and move them in a circular motion while smiling broadly.

## Song Overview

Happy Birthday is the most universally recognized celebration song that transforms any special day into a cherished memory. This short, simple melody makes every child feel genuinely special and valued, supporting emotional development, self-awareness, and social connection. Singing Happy Birthday together as a group reinforces the concept of celebrating others, building community bonds, and creating positive associations with milestones—making it a powerful tool for social-emotional learning and family/classroom traditions.

**Category**: Nursery Rhymes

**Origin**: The original melody comes from the song "Good Morning to All" (1893), composed by Patty Smith Hill and Mildred J. Hill as a classroom greeting song. The lyrics were changed to "Happy Birthday" sometime in the early 1900s, and the song was first published with the birthday lyrics in 1924. It has since become the most sung song in the English language, recognized in nearly every country and culture worldwide.

**Duration**: About 30-45 seconds

## Cultural Notes

While widely recognized in Western cultures, Happy Birthday has been adapted into numerous languages and modified to fit cultural celebration styles. In some countries, different birthday songs are equally or more popular—for example, "Cumpleaños Feliz" in Spanish-speaking countries or various traditional songs in Asian cultures. The familiar melody makes it easy for people of all ages to join in, and the personalization with the child's name makes each celebration feel individual and special. Many modern variations add silly verses, make-up lines, or incorporate local traditions, making it a flexible song that adapts to each family or community's unique celebration style.
