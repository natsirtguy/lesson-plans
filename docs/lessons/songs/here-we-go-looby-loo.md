## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **LOOBY LOO** - A playful, made-up word signed with alternating index and middle fingers bouncing in circles at the side of the head while making a silly expression, then open both hands moving out and together in a circular pattern to show movement and fun

- **HAND** - One hand with fingers together and slightly curled, palm facing down, taps the back of the other arm from wrist to elbow (showing hand location), or flatten palm and tap wrist and forearm

- **FOOT** - Point downward with index finger to the ground or tap the floor with your foot; can also flatten one hand palm-down at shin level and tap upward

- **IN** - Make a "C" shape with fingers and push that hand into an "O" shape made by the other hand, showing something entering or going inside

- **OUT** - Start with one hand making an "O" inside the other hand's "O" shape, then pull the hand out with an "S" or flat hand moving away

- **TURN/AROUND** - Extend both index fingers pointing up, then rotate wrists so hands make a full circular motion, showing a turning or spinning action

- **SHAKE** - Make loose fists and wiggle both hands rapidly back and forth with energy and movement

- **NIGHT** - Bring right hand down across the body from left shoulder to right hip in an arc, with fingers spread, showing darkness or the sun going down; can also make "N" handshape and move it down

## Song Overview

"Here We Go Looby Loo" is a dynamic, participatory circle song that combines music with full-body movement and spatial awareness. Young children ages 2-4+ absolutely love this song because it gives them a clear, predictable structure while allowing them to move their whole body in silly and exciting ways. The repetitive pattern builds confidence as children anticipate which body part comes next, while the "shake it all about" movement provides joyful physical release. This song supports gross motor development, body awareness, bilateral coordination, rhythm skills, and social connection through group participation.

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: "Here We Go Looby Loo" is a traditional British/American children's song with roots in the late 19th century. It evolved from circle games and ring dances, with some musicologists suggesting connections to older folk traditions. The exact authorship is unknown, but the song became popularized through children's songbooks and playgrounds throughout the 20th century. It is sometimes attributed as a variation of the earlier "Looby Loo" or related to folk circle dances.

**Duration**: About 3-5 minutes, depending on how many verses you sing and whether you repeat the chorus after each body-part verse

## Cultural Notes

"Here We Go Looby Loo" has been sung and danced in playgrounds across English-speaking countries for generations, with various cultural adaptations. In Canada and Australia, the song is equally well-known and often sung in similar circle-dance formats. Some versions replace "Saturday night" with "every day" or "all night long" depending on regional traditions. The song's structure mirrors many traditional circle dances found across European cultures, where movement-based songs served as social bonding tools for children. In some contemporary early childhood settings, educators have adapted the song to celebrate different cultures by incorporating movements or instruments from various traditions, making it a bridge between cultural experiences while maintaining the joyful participatory nature that makes it timeless.
