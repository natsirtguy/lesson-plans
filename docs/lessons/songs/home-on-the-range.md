# SONG: Home on the Range

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
O give me a home, where the buffalo roam,
Where the deer and the antelope play,
Where seldom is heard a discouraging word,
And the skies are not cloudy all day.

**Chorus:**
Home, home on the range,
Where the deer and the antelope play,
Where seldom is heard a discouraging word,
And the skies are not cloudy all day.

**Verse 2:**
How often at night when the heavens are bright,
With the light from the glittering stars,
Have I stood there amazed and asked as I gazed,
If their glory exceeds that of ours.

**Chorus:**
Home, home on the range,
Where the deer and the antelope play,
Where seldom is heard a discouraging word,
And the skies are not cloudy all day.

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **HOME** - Point both index fingers together in front of your chest, then move them outward to the sides, making an arc shape to show a house or dwelling
- **BUFFALO** - Make horns with both hands above your head (index and middle fingers extended), then move them forward and slightly outward to show the animal's shape
- **DEER** - Hold both hands near the sides of your head with fingers pointing up to represent the antlers, then gently move them up and down
- **PLAY** - Make "P" shapes with both hands and move them up and down playfully in front of your chest
- **RANGE** - Extend both arms out to the sides with palms down, then sweep them across to show a wide, open area
- **SKY** - Point upward with one hand, then sweep it across to show the expanse of sky above
- **DAY** - Point to the side with one arm extended, representing the path of the sun across the sky during the day

## Song Overview

"Home on the Range" captures the beauty and freedom of the American frontier in a way that resonates with young children who love animals and outdoor adventures. The repetition of the chorus and the vivid imagery of buffalo, deer, and antelope help children develop language and memory skills while fostering an appreciation for nature and wide-open spaces. This gentle, contemplative song is perfect for quieter learning times or as a bridge between active play and rest, and it introduces children to American folk heritage and cultural history.

**Category**: Folk & Traditional

**Origin**: Written in 1873 by Dr. Brewster M. Higley with music composed by Dan Kelly. It originated during the American frontier era and became an iconic symbol of ranching life and the Great Plains. The song was inspired by life in Kansas during the cattle ranching period.

**Duration**: About 2-3 minutes (if singing both verses and chorus)

## Cultural Notes

"Home on the Range" is one of the most recognizable American folk songs and was officially named the official state song of Kansas in 1947. The song reflects the values of 19th-century settlers who were drawn to the vast, open landscapes of the American West.
