# SONG: John Jacob Jingleheimer Schmidt

## Lyrics

**Verse 1 (Normal volume):**
John Jacob Jingleheimer Schmidt,
His name is my name too!
Whenever we go out,
The people always shout,
"There goes John Jacob Jingleheimer Schmidt!"

**Verse 2 (Louder volume):**
JOHN JACOB JINGLEHEIMER SCHMIDT,
HIS NAME IS MY NAME TOO!
WHENEVER WE GO OUT,
THE PEOPLE ALWAYS SHOUT,
"THERE GOES JOHN JACOB JINGLEHEIMER SCHMIDT!"

**Verse 3 (Even louder/shouting):**
JOHN JACOB JINGLEHEIMER SCHMIDT!!!
HIS NAME IS MY NAME TOO!!!
WHENEVER WE GO OUT,
THE PEOPLE ALWAYS SHOUT,
"THERE GOES JOHN JACOB JINGLEHEIMER SCHMIDT!!!"

*(Note: This song is often repeated multiple times with the volume increasing each time, and can include variations where caregivers substitute children's own names)*

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **NAME** - Hold both hands up in front of your body with index and middle fingers extended (like making a "V"). Bring hands together and touch the sides of your head with the two fingers on each side.

- **MY** - Place your open hand over your heart/chest area, then point inward toward yourself.

- **PEOPLE** - Make alternate circles with both index fingers in front of your body, moving them forward, then bring both hands together in front of you, opening them outward to show a group.

- **SHOUT** - Cup one or both hands near your mouth and move them forward and outward with a forceful motion to indicate projecting your voice outward loudly.

- **GO/OUT** - Point two index fingers forward and move them away from your body in an outward direction, as if something is leaving.

- **THERE** - Point forward with your index finger at a location in front of you, indicating a specific place or person.

- **JINGLE** - Hold both hands up with all fingers extended and wiggle them while moving hands side to side in a playful, jingling motion.

## Song Overview

"John Jacob Jingleheimer Schmidt" is a hilarious, tongue-twisting song that young children absolutely love because of its silly, hard-to-pronounce name and the delightful tradition of getting progressively louder with each verse. The repetitive structure with increasing volume helps children develop vocal control, listen to dynamics, and build confidence in their singing voice. This song is perfect for active play times, circle time, transitions, or anytime you need an energy boost. Young children love the silliness and the opportunity to shout together, making it a wonderful tool for self-expression, group participation, and learning about volume and sound levels while maintaining a playful, joyful experience.

**Category**: Nursery Rhymes

**Origin**: This is an American children's song that gained popularity in the mid-20th century, likely emerging from schoolyard and summer camp traditions. While the exact composer is unknown, the song was widely popularized through children's music recordings in the 1960s-1970s. It became a staple in American elementary schools and camps, where the tradition of singing it progressively louder became the standard performance style.

**Duration**: About 1-2 minutes per full repetition (3-5 minutes if sung multiple times with increasing volume)

## Cultural Notes

This song is primarily known in North America and English-speaking countries as a beloved playground and classroom song with universal appeal. The humor of having an unusual name and the exaggeration of the crowd's reaction make it relatable across different cultures and communities. In many schools, educators adapt the song by substituting children's own names during circle time, creating a personalized version that helps young learners feel included and celebrated. The tradition of progressively raising the volume has become the signature way to perform this song and serves as a wonderful opportunity to teach children about volume control, listening skills, and the fun of group participation. Many families around the world have discovered this song through American popular culture and create their own versions with local names and variations.
