# SONG: Little Bo Peep

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
Little Bo Peep has lost her sheep,
And can't tell where to find them;
Leave them alone, and they'll come home,
Bringing their tails behind them.

**Verse 2:**
Little Bo Peep fell fast asleep,
And dreamt she heard them bleating;
But when she woke, she found a joke,
That they were still a-fleeting.

**Verse 3:**
Then up she took her little crook,
Determined for to find them;
She found them indeed, but it made her heart bleed,
For they'd left all their tails behind them.

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **Bo Peep** - Hold both hands near your face with palms facing inward, then open your hands outward as if peeking around something or looking to find something
- **Sheep** - Use both hands to make a scratching or scrubbing motion on your arms to show the woolly texture of sheep's fleece
- **Lost** - Hold both hands out to the sides with palms up and a confused expression to show something is missing or lost
- **Home** - Place your right hand flat against your chest, then bring your left hand up to touch your right hand to show returning to home (self)
- **Sleep** - Rest your tilted head on both your hands pressed together, like sleeping, to show the action of sleeping
- **Tail** - Starting at the base of your spine, move your hand downward and backward with a wagging or swishing motion to show a tail
- **Find** - Hold one hand open with palm up, then bring your other hand down into it with determination to show finding or discovering something
- **Cry/Bleed** - Trace a tear down your cheek with your index finger to show sadness or heartbreak

## Song Overview

Little Bo Peep is a pastoral nursery rhyme with themes of independence, trust, and gentle problem-solving that resonate with young children's emerging social-emotional learning. The character of a young shepherdess tending sheep captures children's imagination and interest in nature and animals. This rhyme supports language development through storytelling, introduces concepts like trust and letting go, and helps children explore emotions like worry and joy through the character's journey of losing and finding her sheep.

**Category**: Nursery Rhymes

**Origin**: Little Bo Peep is one of the oldest surviving English nursery rhymes, with evidence of the verse existing since at least the 16th century. Early versions appeared in collections like "Tom Thumb's Song Book" (1744). ## Cultural Notes

Little Bo Peep has been beloved across English-speaking countries for centuries and has inspired countless adaptations, illustrations, and retellings. The rhyme appears frequently in classic children's book collections and has been featured in numerous films, toys, and merchandise. Different cultures and regions maintain slightly different versions of the verses, though the core story remains consistent. The character of Bo Peep has become part of nursery rhyme canon, sometimes appearing alongside other characters like Mary, Little Miss Muffet, and Little Boy Blue in illustrated collections. The rhyme's gentle message about letting things happen naturally and the reassurance that lost things often return home makes it particularly meaningful for children experiencing separation or transition, helping caregivers discuss these important feelings in an age-appropriate way.
