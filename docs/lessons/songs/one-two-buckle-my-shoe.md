# SONG: One Two Buckle My Shoe

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
One, two, buckle my shoe
Three, four, knock at the door
Five, six, pick up sticks
Seven, eight, lay them straight
Nine, ten, do it again

**Verse 2:**
Eleven, twelve, dig and delve
Thirteen, fourteen, maids a-courting
Fifteen, sixteen, maids in the kitchen
Seventeen, eighteen, maids in waiting
Nineteen, twenty, my plate's empty

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **One** - Hold up one finger (index finger) on one hand with other fingers closed
- **Two** - Hold up two fingers (index and middle finger) in a V-shape on one hand
- **Buckle** - Make a fist with both hands facing each other at waist level, then twist the hands together in a locking motion to show fastening
- **Shoe** - Clap both hands together sharply with hands positioned at shoe level to represent putting on or fastening shoes
- **Door** - Hold both hands up vertically with palms facing each other (like an open door frame) and move one hand forward and back like opening and closing a door
- **Knock** - Make a fist with one hand and tap it gently onto the palm of your other open hand, repeat several times
- **Sticks** - Hold both hands vertically with index fingers pointing up (like standing sticks) and move them apart to show picking up sticks
- **Straight** - Hold both index fingers up parallel to each other and move them forward in a straight line to show straightness

## Song Overview

"One Two Buckle My Shoe" is a classic counting song that introduces young children to numbers while keeping them entertained with a bouncy rhythm and relatable actions. The predictable pattern of rhyming couplets and the associated actions (buckling shoes, knocking, picking up sticks) help toddlers learn number sequencing while engaging their large and fine motor skills. This song is an excellent tool for early mathematics exposure and develops memory skills through extended verses. It works wonderfully during active play or as a learning-focused activity.

**Category**: Alphabet & Learning

**Origin**: This traditional English nursery rhyme dates back to the early 1600s and is thought to have roots in medieval counting-out games used to select players. The song was popularized in children's literature throughout the 19th and 20th centuries. Various versions exist across English-speaking countries, with some verses added or modified based on regional variations. It remains one of the most widely recognized counting songs in early childhood education.

**Duration**: About 1.5-2 minutes (all verses)

## Cultural Notes

"One Two Buckle My Shoe" is recognized across English-speaking countries including England, Scotland, Ireland, Australia, and North America. The song has inspired numerous variations, with some regions adding region-specific verses or actions. The extended verses featuring activities like digging and cooking reflect the rural and domestic life of earlier centuries. The rhyme connects naturally to daily routines (putting on shoes) making it relatable for toddlers. International adaptations exist where the rhyme scheme and counting methods have been adapted to different languages while maintaining the playful spirit of the original.
