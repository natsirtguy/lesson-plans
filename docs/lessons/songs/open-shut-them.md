# SONG: Open Shut Them

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
Open, shut them
Open, shut them
Give a little clap
Clap, clap, clap
Open, shut them
Open, shut them
Put them in your lap
Lap, lap, lap

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **OPEN** - Start with both hands in fists in front of you at chest level. Spread your fingers wide open while moving your hands slightly apart, as if opening a book.
- **SHUT** - Start with both hands open in front of you with fingers spread. Close all fingers into fists simultaneously, as if closing a door.
- **CLAP** - Bring both hands together in front of your body and tap your palms against each other repeatedly in a clapping motion.
- **HANDS** - Point to or hold up both of your hands in front of you, or make the "hand" shape (flat palm facing down) in front of your body.
- **LAP** - Pat gently on your lap area with both hands, moving them up and down on your legs.
- **GIVE** - Hold your hand out in front of you with an open palm as if offering something, then move it toward another person's open hand.

## Song Overview

"Open Shut Them" is a beloved hand action song that helps young children develop fine motor control, hand-eye coordination, and listening skills. The repetitive, predictable lyrics combined with simple hand movements make it perfect for 2-4 year olds who are just beginning to develop coordination and body awareness. This song is ideal for transitions, movement breaks, sensory play, or anytime you want to help children engage their hands and minds together.

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: Traditional children's song with unknown original authorship, widely used in early childhood education programs since the early 1900s. The song is believed to have originated in the United States and has become a staple in preschools and daycare settings worldwide due to its simplicity and effectiveness for teaching body coordination.

**Duration**: About 1-2 minutes (depending on pace and number of repetitions)

## Cultural Notes

"Open Shut Them" is recognized and sung in early childhood programs across English-speaking countries including the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, and Australia. The song's universal appeal lies in its simplicity and the natural connection between the lyrics and hand movements, making it accessible for children of different backgrounds and abilities. Many variations exist where different actions replace the "clap" and "lap" lines, such as "wave them in the air," "put them on your head," or "tap them side to side," allowing caregivers to adapt the song for different learning goals, energy levels, and developmental needs.
