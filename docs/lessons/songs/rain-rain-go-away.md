# SONG: Rain Rain Go Away

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
Rain, rain, go away
Come again another day
Little Johnny wants to play

(Alternative version uses the child's name instead of "Johnny")

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **Rain** - Hold both hands up above your head with fingers spread, then wiggle all your fingers while moving both hands downward in a gentle falling motion to show rain falling
- **Go** - Point forward with both index fingers and move both hands away from your body in the direction you're pointing, showing movement away
- **Away** - Hold one hand up with palm facing your body, then flick all fingers outward and away from you in a quick motion, showing something going away
- **Come** - Crook the fingers of both hands and pull them toward your body in a beckoning motion, showing "come here"
- **Day** - Hold one arm up and stretched out to one side (like the sun's path), then move it across in front of your body to the other side to show the passage of a day
- **Play** - Make Y-shapes with both hands (thumb and pinky fingers extended) and twist them back and forth at waist level to show a playful, joyful motion
- **Johnny** - Spell out J-O-H-N-N-Y using fingerspelling, or if the child has a name sign established, use that instead

## Song Overview

"Rain Rain Go Away" is a charming, short song that helps toddlers express their feelings about weather and their desire to play outdoors. With its simple three-line structure, this song is easy for young children to learn and repeat. The melody is catchy and easy to remember, making it perfect for emotional expression and weather awareness. Children enjoy the sense of agency—even if the rain doesn't listen—as they "command" it to go away. This song supports language development, emotional vocabulary, and understanding of weather patterns.

**Category**: Nature & Animal Songs

**Origin**: "Rain Rain Go Away" is a traditional English nursery rhyme with origins dating back at least to the 17th century. Its exact authorship is unknown, but it has been documented in various children's collections since the 1800s. The simple, straightforward rhyme and relatable theme made it easy to transmit across generations and cultures. The song became especially popular in the Victorian era and remains a staple of early childhood education worldwide.

**Duration**: About 45 seconds

## Cultural Notes

"Rain Rain Go Away" is known worldwide in English-speaking countries and has been adapted in many languages with similar themes of children expressing wishes about weather. In various cultures, similar chants and songs exist that encourage rain to go away or, conversely, invite rain for crops. Some regions have adapted the song to include local children's names or have created call-and-response versions where multiple children join in. The universal theme of wanting to play outside makes this song transcend cultural boundaries, and variations appear in children's literature from Australia, Canada, the United Kingdom, and the United States, often with slight lyrical modifications based on local contexts and weather patterns.
