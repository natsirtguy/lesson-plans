# SONG: Rock-a-Bye Baby

## Lyrics

**Main Verse:**
Rock-a-bye baby, in the tree top
When the wind blows, the cradle will rock
When the bough breaks, the cradle will fall
And down will come baby, cradle and all

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **BABY** - Rock your arms gently side to side as if cradling an infant, with hands crossed over your chest or in front of you
- **ROCK** - Make a swaying motion with your whole body or your upper body, mimicking the gentle back-and-forth rocking motion of a cradle
- **WIND** - Hold your hands out in front of you with fingers wiggling and make a flowing, pushing motion from one side to the other to show wind blowing
- **TREE** - Extend your arms upward and outward with fingers spread, arms slightly curved to form the shape of a tree
- **FALL** - Hold both hands above your head with fingers pointed down, then drop them down in a falling motion
- **SLEEP** - Tilt your head to rest against your hand pressed against your cheek, as if sleeping
- **CRADLE** - Make a rocking motion with your arms as if gently swinging a baby in a cradle back and forth

## Song Overview

Rock-a-Bye Baby is a timeless lullaby that soothes children with its gentle melody and rhythmic rocking motion. Young toddlers are naturally drawn to the repetitive melody and the imagery of being rocked to sleep, which activates the body's natural calming response. This song is perfect for bedtime routines and helps develop language skills while encouraging parent-child bonding through the physical act of rocking and swaying together.

**Category**: Lullabies

**Origin**: "Rock-a-Bye Baby" is an English lullaby of uncertain origin, with roots likely in 17th-century England. The exact authorship is unknown, though some sources suggest it may have originated from watching birds nesting in tree branches. The song was first published in American context in the late 1800s and has become one of the most recognizable lullabies in English-speaking countries. Despite the somewhat dark imagery of the falling cradle in the lyrics, the gentle melody and soothing rhythm make it universally beloved for calming infants and toddlers.

**Duration**: About 1 minute

## Cultural Notes

Rock-a-Bye Baby is known across English-speaking cultures and has remained virtually unchanged for centuries, making it a shared cultural touchstone for generations of families. The song appears in many cultural traditions and has inspired variations in other languages, such as "Bye Baw, Baw Bunting" in Scottish tradition. Some families have created alternative lyrics to replace the falling cradle imagery with gentler scenes, though the original remains most popular. The song demonstrates how lullabies across cultures share common elements—gentle melodies, rhythmic patterns, and imagery of safety and rest—that help calm children regardless of language or background.
