# SONG: Row Row Row Your Boat

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
Row, row, row your boat,
Gently down the stream,
Merrily, merrily, merrily, merrily,
Life is but a dream.

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **ROW** - Hold both hands in front of you as if holding oars. Make circular rowing motions alternating your hands forward and back, as if rowing a boat.
- **BOAT** - Place both hands together with palms facing up and fingers extended, forming the shape of a boat. Move hands forward together as if the boat is moving through water.
- **STREAM** - Hold one hand open and flat, then make a wavy motion downward with fingers wiggling to represent flowing water.
- **GENTLY** - Hold one hand open and flat, then lower it slowly and smoothly with a calm, gentle motion to show something soft and careful.
- **MERRILY** - Make a big smile, then brush both hands outward from your chest while bouncing slightly, showing happiness and joy.
- **LIFE** - Point to yourself with both index fingers, then move them up the center of your body from waist to chest to show the journey of life.
- **DREAM** - Touch your temple with one finger and open your hand outward near your head, then close and open your eyes slowly to show imagination and dreams.
- **DOWN** - Point your index finger downward in the direction the boat is traveling on the stream.

## Song Overview

"Row Row Row Your Boat" is one of the most beloved children's songs worldwide, known for its simple melody and engaging rowing action. Young children ages 2-4 love the repetitive rhythm and the opportunity to make big rowing motions with their arms, which builds gross motor skills and coordination. This song teaches children about gentle movements, rhythm, following directions, and can be sung as a round (where multiple people start at different times), introducing the concept of harmony and listening to others. It's perfect for movement time, outdoor play, or even bath time!

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: "Row Row Row Your Boat" is a traditional English nursery rhyme and song, believed to have originated in the mid-1800s, though its exact author is unknown. It became internationally famous as a singing round, where multiple singers start at different intervals to create harmony. The song has been translated and adapted in cultures around the world.

**Duration**: About 45-60 seconds for one complete verse; can be repeated or sung as a round

## Cultural Notes

This simple boat song has traveled across cultures and continents, with variations found in many countries. In some cultures, it's paired with water play activities and introduces children to concepts of gentle, coordinated movement. The philosophical message "Life is but a dream" offers a moment of reflection suitable for young minds. The round version teaches children about listening and harmony, making it popular in music education. Many cultures have adapted the lyrics or melody while keeping the essence of the rowing motion that makes this song so physically engaging for toddlers.
