# SONG: Simple Simon

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
Simple Simon met a pieman
Going to the fair,
Says Simple Simon to the pieman,
"Let me taste your ware."
Says the pieman unto Simon,
"Show me first your penny."
Says Simple Simon to the pieman,
"Sir, I haven't any."

**Verse 2:**
Simple Simon met a baker
Going to the fair,
Says Simple Simon to the baker,
"Let me taste your bread."
Says the baker unto Simon,
"Show me first your penny."
Says Simple Simon to the baker,
"Sir, I haven't any."

**Verse 3:**
Simple Simon met a milkmaid
Going to the fair,
Says Simple Simon to the milkmaid,
"Let me taste your milk."
Says the milkmaid unto Simon,
"Show me first your penny."
Says Simple Simon to the milkmaid,
"Sir, I haven't any."

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **SIMPLE** - Hold one hand up with fingers apart and move it in a small circular motion, or make a straightforward gesture showing something uncomplicated
- **MEET** - Extend both index fingers or hands forward and bring them together in the middle, touching at the fingertips
- **FAIR** - Hold both hands with fingers spread, palms facing out, and move them outward and upward in a sweeping motion to show something exciting and big
- **TASTE** - Point your index finger to your lips or tongue to show the sense of taste
- **PENNY** - Hold thumb and index finger together on one hand showing a small circular object, then place it on your palm
- **BREAD** - Hold your left arm across your body horizontally, then use your right hand to make downward slicing motions across it
- **MILK** - Hold both hands in front of you with fingers curled and thumbs out (like holding a cow), and squeeze the hands in a milking motion

## Song Overview

"Simple Simon" is a narrative nursery rhyme that tells a delightful story of a character making repeated attempts to get food without payment. Children love the repetitive structure and the silly character who keeps trying the same unsuccessful approach. This rhyme supports language development through dialogue and introduces concepts like cause-and-effect, money, and marketplace interactions in an age-appropriate way that sparks imagination.

**Category**: Nursery Rhymes

**Origin**: "Simple Simon" dates back to the 18th century and appears in traditional nursery rhyme collections. The rhyme reflects historical marketplace culture and the concept of trading for goods. It has been continuously popular in English-speaking countries and has inspired numerous variations, adaptations, and games over the centuries.

**Duration**: About 1-1.5 minutes (depending on how many verses you sing)

## Cultural Notes

This rhyme appears in nursery rhyme collections across English-speaking cultures and has inspired variations featuring different vendors and items. The marketplace setting reflects historical fair culture where families would gather to buy food and goods. Some cultures have created their own versions featuring local vendors or foods relevant to their regions.
