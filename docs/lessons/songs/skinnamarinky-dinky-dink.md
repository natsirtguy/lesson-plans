## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **LOVE** - Cross both hands over your heart, palms inward, then open them outward in a giving motion. This shows affection flowing from your heart.
- **YOU** - Extend one arm outward with your index finger pointing toward the person you're singing to. This direct gesture of connection works well when directed at the child or caregiver.
- **ME/I** - Point your index finger at your own chest with one hand. Use this when singing about yourself or making the song personal to the child.
- **HEART** - Make a heart shape by crossing your two thumbs and index fingers together to form a heart outline over your chest. A favorite among young children.
- **HUG** - Wrap both arms around yourself in a hugging motion, swaying gently side to side. Perfect for showing warmth and affection during the song.
- **DINK** - Make a playful bouncing motion with your pointer and middle fingers together on your other palm, or gently tap your palm. This silly sign matches the nonsense word's playful nature.
- **HAND/WITH** - Hold out both open palms facing each other and then bring them together. This works for expressing togetherness and "with all my heart."

## Song Overview

"Skinnamarinky Dinky Dink" is a joyful celebration of love and affection that resonates deeply with young children. The silly, made-up syllables ("skidamarink a dink a dink") make the song fun to sing and remember, while the clear message "I love you" gives children language for expressing emotion. This song supports emotional development, language acquisition, and social bonding while naturally incorporating hand motions and body awareness. It's perfect for circle time, one-on-one bonding moments, or anytime you want to reinforce feelings of love and security with the children in your care.

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: Written in the 1920s by Charles Newell Barnard and published in "The Golden Book of Favorite Songs." The song became widely known to modern audiences through its featured use on the children's television program "Barney & Friends" in the 1990s, though it has roots in early 20th-century children's music and has been sung by generations of families.

**Duration**: About 1-2 minutes
