# SONG: Teddy Bear Teddy Bear

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
Teddy bear, teddy bear, turn around,
Teddy bear, teddy bear, touch the ground,
Teddy bear, teddy bear, reach up high,
Teddy bear, teddy bear, touch the sky.

**Verse 2:**
Teddy bear, teddy bear, turn out the light,
Teddy bear, teddy bear, say good night,
Teddy bear, teddy bear, go to bed,
Teddy bear, teddy bear, rest your head.

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **Teddy Bear** - Make a claw shape with both hands and hug them across your chest as if hugging a stuffed animal.
- **Turn Around** - Make a circular motion with both index fingers, showing the body turning in a complete circle.
- **Ground** - Hold both hands flat with palms down and point them downward to show the ground or floor.
- **Reach** - Extend both arms upward and forward as if reaching toward something just out of reach.
- **High** - Hold one hand high above your head, palm facing up, to indicate something being high up.
- **Sky** - Hold your arm up and sweep it across above your head from left to right to show the expansive sky.
- **Bed** - Hold both hands flat and together near your cheek, tilting your head onto them to show sleeping or resting in bed.
- **Sleep** (or Rest Your Head) - Bring both hands together and place them against your cheek, tilting your head to one side as if sleeping peacefully.

## Song Overview

Teddy Bear, Teddy Bear is an interactive action song that transforms a beloved stuffed animal into a movement guide for young children, making exercise and body awareness playful and engaging. The song's simple, repetitive commands ("turn around," "touch the ground," "reach up high") give 2-4 year olds clear physical instructions to follow, supporting their developing gross motor skills, body coordination, and the ability to understand and follow directions. The predictable structure and the warm, comforting presence of the teddy bear character make this song ideal for active play times, transitions between activities, or as part of a morning movement routine that helps children wake up their bodies in a joyful way.

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: This is a modern nursery rhyme and action song that became popular in the 20th century in English-speaking countries; the exact author is unknown. It grew organically through early childhood education settings and playgrounds, where generations of children have used it as a jumping rhyme and movement game. While the exact origins are unclear, the song's enduring popularity suggests it emerged from the collective creativity of parents and educators seeking engaging ways to encourage movement and following instructions.

**Duration**: About 1 to 1.5 minutes

## Cultural Notes

Teddy Bear, Teddy Bear is recognized primarily in English-speaking cultures, particularly in North America and the United Kingdom, where it remains a staple of playgrounds, jump rope games, and early childhood classrooms. The song often varies slightly from region to region and community to community, with some versions including additional verses about cooking and other activities. The inclusion of the teddy bear character reflects the cultural importance of stuffed animals in Western childhood, and the song's enduring presence suggests it serves an important developmental role in helping children learn to follow directions through joyful, embodied play. Many caregivers remember singing this song during their own childhood, making it a wonderful way to pass down intergenerational traditions.
