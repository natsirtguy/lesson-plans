## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **BED** - Place both hands together at an angle against your cheek to indicate sleeping/resting position
- **ROLL** - Make two fists and rotate them around each other as if rolling an object
- **FELL** - Hold hand high and drop it downward quickly to show something falling
- **OUT** - Start with hand inside, palm down, then move it outward and upward
- **SCREAM** - Open mouth wide and move hand from throat outward to show a scream
- **SHOUT** - Cup hand near mouth and push outward as if projecting voice
- **REMEMBER** - Tap your forehead with index finger, then bring hands together
- **PAJAMAS** - Trace the outline of clothing/buttons down your chest with both index fingers

## Song Overview

"Ten in the Bed" is a delightful countdown song that captivates young children with its repetitive actions, silly humor, and engaging narrative. This song powerfully supports number recognition and counting backward through a fun, active experience where children can physically roll and move along. The predictable structure combined with the interactive "roll over" motions helps develop rhythm, coordination, anticipation skills, and body awarenessâ€”making it perfect for active playtime while also building mathematical thinking through numbers and sequence.

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: Traditional British nursery rhyme and children's song, popularized in the early-to-mid 20th century. The song has been featured in countless children's books, recordings, and educational programs, becoming a worldwide classic. The addition of the "tie a knot in your pajamas" line adds humor that appeals to young listeners.

**Duration**: About 3-4 minutes

## Cultural Notes

This song appears in various cultural adaptations around the world, with some versions featuring different animals instead of people in the bed. The humor in the repeated warning to "tie a knot in your pajamas" resonates across cultures and age groups, making it engaging for both young children and older siblings. Many families create their own verse variations, turning it into a personalized family tradition. The song's emphasis on bedtime routines also aligns with healthy sleep hygiene practices that caregivers across cultures recognize as important for child development.
