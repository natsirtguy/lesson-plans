# SONG: The Farmer in the Dell

The farmer in the dell, the farmer in the dell
Hi-ho the derry-o, the farmer in the dell

**Verse 2:**

**Verse 7:**

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **FARMER** - Place both hands in a "claw" shape near your chest and move them outward alternately as if working the soil, or sign FARM + ER (person)
- **WIFE** - Touch your cheek with thumb of "A" hand, then join hands together to show togetherness
- **CHILD** - Hold hand flat, palm down, and move it to show a child's height or bend fingers to indicate youth
- **DOG** - Snap fingers twice on each hand, or hold hands up with thumbs on temples and bounce them to show dog's floppy ears
- **CAT** - Touch the sides of your face near eyes with both hands (showing whiskers) and pull hands outward
- **RAT** - Make "R" shape with both hands and move them near nose, or rub your nose with index and middle finger together
- **CHEESE** - Press heel of one hand into palm of other, then twist hands apart to show cheese wedge shape
- **ALONE** - Make "A" shape with both hands, move them in circles around each other, then bring one hand to touch the other

## Song Overview

"The Farmer in the Dell" is a beloved traditional game-song that combines music with playful social interaction and imaginative storytelling. This song beautifully supports language development, rhythm, and social-emotional learning through its repetitive lyrics and circular game format where children physically interact with one another. The cumulative structure (each character "takes" the next) helps develop sequencing and memory skills, while the collaborative game element builds social bonding and teaches children about family relationships and community.

**Category**: Folk & Traditional

**Origin**: German folk song originally titled "Der Bauer im Walde" (The Farmer in the Forest), which traveled to America and the English-speaking world in the 19th century. It became widely adopted as a children's singing game, with regional variations in both lyrics and game rules across different English-speaking countries. The "Hi-ho the derry-o" refrain is particularly associated with the English-language adaptation.

**Duration**: About 3-4 minutes

## Cultural Notes

This song serves as a traditional children's circle game across many Western cultures, with specific rules varying by region and community. In some cultures, it's called "Green Groves" or has modified lyrics featuring different occupations or animals.
