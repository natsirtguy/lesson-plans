# SONG: The Itsy Bitsy Spider

## Lyrics

**Verse 1:**
The itsy bitsy spider climbed up the water spout
Down came the rain and washed the spider out
Out came the sun and dried up all the rain
And the itsy bitsy spider climbed up the spout again

**Verse 2:**
The itsy bitsy spider climbed up the window sill
Down came the rain and gave him quite a chill
Out came the sun all warm and dry
And the itsy bitsy spider climbed toward the sky

**Verse 3:**
The itsy bitsy spider climbed up the maple tree
Down came the rain so wet and cold, you see
Out came the sun, it shone so bright
And the itsy bitsy spider climbed on with all his might

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **SPIDER** - Touch both thumbs and index fingers together, then move them apart in a walking motion, alternating which hand leads. Repeat the motion to show the spider moving.

- **CLIMB/CLIMBED** - Place hands in front of you with fingers hooked, then move them upward alternately, as if climbing a rope or ladder.

- **WATER** - Hold all four fingers and thumb together on one hand, then tap the side of your chin twice. This is the sign for water.

- **RAIN** - Hold both hands above your head with fingers spread and slightly bent, then wiggle all fingers while moving both hands downward in a bouncing motion.

- **DOWN** - Point your index finger downward and move your hand downward. You can also use both hands moving downward together.

- **SUN** - Make a circle with your thumb and index finger above your head (like you're framing the sun), then move the circle outward.

- **OUT** - Hold one hand in front of you with fingers together, then brush the back of your other hand outward through the palm, moving away from your body.

## Song Overview

"The Itsy Bitsy Spider" is one of the most beloved children's songs, perfect for developing language, coordination, and imagination. Young children are naturally delighted by the finger movements that accompany the lyrics, making this an ideal action song that combines storytelling with fine motor skill practice. The song's simple, repetitive structure helps children follow along and remember the lyrics, while the visual imagery of a tiny spider climbing up a spout captivates toddler imagination. The circular nature of the song (the spider climbs again) teaches cause-and-effect relationships in a fun, engaging way.

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: This traditional nursery rhyme has unknown exact origins, though it became widely known in the early 1900s. It gained significant popularity after being illustrated by Kate Greenaway in 1910. The song has roots in English children's literature and has become one of the most universally recognized children's songs worldwide. The finger movements that accompany the song are a modern addition that developed as the song spread through schools and childcare settings.

**Duration**: About 1-2 minutes per verse (typically 2-3 minutes for the full song with all verses)

## Cultural Notes

"The Itsy Bitsy Spider" exists in many variations around the world, with different cultures adapting the lyrics and sometimes changing the climbed object (window sill, maple tree, rain gutter, etc.). In some English-speaking regions, it's known as "The Eensy Weensy Spider" or "The Incy Wincy Spider." The finger movements that accompany this song are universal and transcend language barriers, making it accessible to children regardless of their native language.
