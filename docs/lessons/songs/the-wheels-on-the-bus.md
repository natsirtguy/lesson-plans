## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **WHEELS** - Make two fists and rotate them forward in circles to show the wheels turning, one circle then the other, like pedaling a bicycle.

- **BUS** - Hold both hands in an open position at the sides of your body to outline the shape of a large bus, then move them forward slightly.

- **ROUND** - Use your index fingers to trace a circle in the air in front of you, moving in a complete circular motion.

- **OPEN** - Hold both hands together in front of you with palms facing each other, then open them apart like opening a door or a book.

- **SHUT** - With hands open in front of you, bring them together to close them, like closing a book or door.

- **BEEP** - Make a fist and tap it twice on your chest or hand, mimicking the sound of a horn.

- **SWISH** - Hold one hand out with fingers together and move it side to side in a swishing motion, like windshield wipers moving back and forth.

- **UP/DOWN** - Point your index finger upward above your head for "up," then point downward below your waist for "down," showing the directional opposite movements.

## Song Overview

"The Wheels on the Bus" is a beloved action-packed song that combines movement, repetition, and sensory engagement to capture young children's imaginations. Children love the familiar sounds and predictable patterns, plus the opportunity to make sounds like "beep," "swish," and "wah" alongside caregivers. This song supports language development through repeated lyrics, gross motor skills through hand and body movements, and turn-taking as different verses give children opportunities to participate with different actions.

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: "The Wheels on the Bus" was written in 1946 by Verna Hills and popularized through various recordings and children's media. It draws from American school bus experiences and has become one of the most recognizable children's songs worldwide, beloved for its simplicity, humor, and endless potential for creative verses and gestures.

**Duration**: About 3-5 minutes depending on how many verses are included and sung at tempo
