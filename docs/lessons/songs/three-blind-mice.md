# SONG: Three Blind Mice

## Lyrics

Three blind mice
See how they run
They all ran after the farmer's wife
Who cut off their tails with a carving knife
Did you ever see such a thing in your life
As three blind mice

**Verse 3:**
The farmer's wife was cutting bread
Three blind mice ran round her head
They scampered here and they scampered there
Three blind mice were everywhere
Did you ever see such a sight
As three blind mice

## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **Three** - Hold up your dominant hand and extend your index, middle, and ring fingers while keeping your other fingers curled down to show the number three
- **Blind** - Hold both index fingers in an "X" shape in front of your eyes or cross one hand across your eyes to show blindness
- **Mice** - Hold your hand up near your head and wiggle your index and middle fingers downward in a quick, scurrying motion to show a small mouse running
- **Run** - Hold both hands in loose fists in front of you and move them back and forth rapidly as if running
- **Farmer** - Make a "C" shape and rub it down your chest to show overalls or farming clothes, then place your hand flat and palm down, moving it outward to show the land
- **Wife** - Touch your thumb to your chin, then move your hand down to your chest to show a woman or wife
- **Knife** - Hold your index finger up like a blade and make a cutting motion across the palm of your other hand
- **Cut** - Open your hand like scissors with your index and middle fingers extended, and snap them together in a cutting motion

## Song Overview

One of the oldest and most famous English nursery rhymes, "Three Blind Mice" features a bouncy, memorable melody and rhythmic language that helps develop phonological awareness and listening skills in young children. Despite its darkly humorous storyline, the sing-song quality of the verse and its predictable rhythm make it engaging and fun for toddlers learning language patterns and early reading skills. The repetitive structure supports memory development and provides excellent opportunities for call-and-response singing between caregivers and children.

**Category**: Nursery Rhymes

**Origin**: Published in 1609 in John Playford's "The English Dancing Master," this is one of the oldest surviving English nursery rhymes. ## Cultural Notes

"Three Blind Mice" is recognized worldwide, particularly in English-speaking countries, and has become one of the most iconic children's rhymes globally. The melody has been adapted and reused in numerous songs, including the satirical "Rub-a-dub-dub, three men in a tub." Many cultures have created their own adaptations featuring different animals and situations while maintaining the bouncy, rhythmic structure that makes the song so memorable. The song has been featured in countless children's media adaptations, video games, and educational programs, cementing its place as a universal cultural touchstone for early childhood learning across generations and continents.
