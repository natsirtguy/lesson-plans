## ASL Signs

Learn these signs to use while singing:

- **WHERE** - Hold both hands in front of you with palms up and shoulders raised slightly, then shake your hands slightly outward while raising eyebrows in a questioning expression.

- **THUMBKIN** - Hold up your thumb on both hands (all other fingers down) at chest level, moving them slightly to show which finger is being named.

- **HERE** - Point with both index fingers to the space in front of you at chest level, palms facing up, making a small circular motion.

- **HELLO** - Bring your open right hand up to the right side of your forehead, then move it outward in a small arc, as if waving a greeting.

- **THANK YOU** - Bring the tips of your right fingers to your chin and then move your hand outward and down toward the person you're thanking, palm facing upward.

- **RUN AWAY** - Hook both index fingers and move them quickly away from your body in a running motion, wiggling all fingers as they move.

- **WELL/GOOD** - Bring your right open hand to your mouth, fingertips touching your lips, then move it forward and down in an arc to end with your palm facing up.

## Song Overview

"Where Is Thumbkin" is a beloved finger play song that combines music with motor skill development and interactive learning. Children love the predictable pattern and the excitement of watching each finger "appear" and "run away." This song is perfect for developing fine motor control, finger identification, language repetition, and social interaction skills. It's ideal for circle time, transition moments, or whenever children need an engaging activity that requires no materials.

**Category**: Action Songs

**Origin**: This traditional children's song has uncertain exact origins but has been a staple in English-speaking early childhood education for over a century. It's a variation of the finger play tradition that dates back to at least the 19th century. Different cultures have similar finger play songs with comparable structures and purposes.

**Duration**: About 3-4 minutes (all five verses)

## Cultural Notes

"Where Is Thumbkin" is known and sung in many English-speaking countries including the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, and Australia, often as part of classic nursery rhyme collections. Some international variations use different finger names or slightly modified lyrics, but the core concept of a question-and-answer song with finger motions remains consistent. This song reflects the universal appeal of interactive finger play across cultures, serving similar developmental purposes in early childhood education worldwide. Similar finger play songs exist in other languages, such as variations in German, Spanish, and French-speaking countries, showing how this interactive learning method transcends cultural boundaries.
